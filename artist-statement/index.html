Artist Statement

The End?

In Synthesis 2 I drew ideas off of my previous data set (the Million Song Dataset) that I used to create a word cloud of popular words in song titles, which you can see here. The goal for this project was to produce a line graph — in Python — to compare the number of titles that contained the word “love” by year. I did indeed produce a graph; it was the line part that proved to be quite difficult.

Simply because of time restraints (as I’m sure everyone can relate), I was unable to troubleshoot the graph and produce a working line. I am however, very proud of being able to accomplish the following: read a CSV file into a Jupyter notebook, make a list out of data cells from individual columns, remove certain values (like the year 0) from a list, join lists together, and convert types of variables (Strings, floats, lists, etc.) into other data types. Maybe I do know more than I think I do but just take more time that others to show it. Maybe I am even OK with that.

Another issue altogether is Github. I was so excited because I thought I finally understood how to fully utilize it — in terms of this project, anyway. I was doing great; I cloned a repository, added stuff to it, committed it, and… it wouldn’t let me push it to my master branch. Which I was confused about. It made it seem like I wanted to push it to YOUR master branch, which obviously I didn’t want; I had the repository forked and cloned, which led me to believe that I would push to MY repository, but my computer had other ideas, I guess. Github still remains a mystery to me, for now.

I also switched my HTML and CSS up a bit. I did use the basic skeleton that I utilized in the Skills Development and Synthesis 1 assignments, but I tried to use divs instead of one-cell tables for the text (which didn’t really yield the results that I particularly wanted, but sometimes that’s OK!) as well as switched up the navigation bar thanks to this tutorial — though I didn’t opt for the animation option because I didn’t really think it would “fit” in with the feel of the site.

I pride myself on being brutally honest, delightfully sassy, and just charming enough to convince people to doing what I want them to do — most of the time. So, for the rest of this statement, I will be exactly all of those things. This project is a flop, garbage even, and I know that. But there’s a bigger issue here. College is awful! Life is unfair! What even is the point of trying to succeed in these things?!

Which is how I’ve been feeling for literally all of my post-secondary education and it is only getting worse as time passes. Full disclosure, I was so anxious during the review session that I literally thought I was going to vomit all over the lab. I was maybe seconds away but I maintained my composure. This, as you can expect, made the rest of the day exceedingly difficult; now, imagine this feeling every day. That is my life as a student.

This isn’t me complaining (OK, maybe a little bit), but that’s just sad and messed up. Lately, college has only been about test scores and GPA, which are things that shouldn’t define a person, instead of their personality or other things that are more accurate descriptions. This is getting rambly, so I’ll stop — even though writing seems to be one of the few skills I still possess.

So, even though this project isn’t exactly what I wanted it to be, it’s done. I’m happy (that it’s over). I just genuinely hope that I have something to contribute to my team for the Team Assignment so I don’t let them down like I constantly let everyone else in my life, including myself, down.
