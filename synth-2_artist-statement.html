Artist Statement

I am quite a bit prouder of this second data visualization assignment. I created my own dataset, if a small, incomplete one, and my understanding of how to use the tutorials to work with matplotlib certainly improved. Most importantly, I think my subject is more interesting, and also worthier of being pursued with additional resources.

My project looks at the rise of populism in Europe compared to the rise of recorded instances of hate crimes. It uses the percentage results of recent elections and hate crime data recorded by the Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights. I would either say it complicates the commonplace, depending on how solidified someone’s feelings about the “truth” of populism are, or is a subject from history, if you consider the last couple years as (recent) history. It charts the populist percentage of the vote of five countries, Austria, France, Germany, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom, as they are some of the most prominent in recent news regarding elections and populism. It then compares by turn the country’s hate crime data in a separate line graph.

Several problems immediately arose when I was compiling my data set. Fist, time. I would have loved to have far more countries represented, perhaps creating a chloropleth that, in increasing shades of red, represented the populist vote all over Europe. Although that would be cool, alarming, and probably misleading, I couldn’t find a data set like that for all of Europe, and did not have enough time to create one. Also, the infrequency and varied timing of elections made it difficult to compare these results. As can be seen in my original dataset, there are several years that go by without any election. I ended up having to repeat the number from one year until the next election in order for the graph to make sense. The hate crime data was another difficulty, as the numbers were very inconsistent, likely because definitions and recording practices for hate crimes in different countries are also inconsistent. I don’t know enough of statistics to be able to make a comparison, nor do I really think a comparison in one graph would be valid, so I separated them.

I followed the tutorial on our GitHub that demonstrated how to create a matplotlib line graph, with only a few modifications. Going through line-by-line and reading the comments, then only writing the line myself once I understood what it did, was a real help to my learning process. I had a bit of an issue printing the images, as savefig(), which had worked on my last project, just saved a blank page. I eventually just used a screenshot, so the quality is a bit low. My one real regret was not having enough time to devote to using JavaScript to make my site interactive. Right now the page uses links to travel between hate crime data for each country, which is inconvenient for loading and causes a lot more scrolling than I’d like.

I believe, if my team is interested, we could take this concept even farther, like the site which I got most of my data from, http://www.cnn.com/2016/12/03/europe/populism-in-europe-visual-guide/, which included immigration and GDP data. I think it’s definitely a topic that is relevant and worthwhile, if we have the time to create an accurate dataset and more valid, less misrepresenting graphs for it.
